What is a piano?

A piano is a large keyboard instrument with 88 black and white keys that, when pressed, trigger hammers to strike strings, producing sound; its name comes from "pianoforte," meaning "soft-loud," because its volume can vary from soft to loud depending on key pressure, making it incredibly versatile for many musical genres. Invented by Bartolomeo Cristofori around 1700, it features a cast-iron frame, strings, a soundboard for amplification, and pedals for tonal effects, evolving into grand and upright forms
